# Diagnostic Assessments

## The Importance of Diagnostic Assessments

Diagnostic assessments (pre-assessments) provide instructors with information about students' prior knowledge and misconceptions before beginning a learning activity. They also diagnose students' strengths and areas of need. This serves two key purposes:

1. **Effectiveness**: Understanding students' prior knowledge allows you to begin teaching at an appropriate starting point and providing instruction and scaffolding within students' Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD).
2. **Misconceptions**: If students have misconceptions, these will prevent them from building correct understandings of new content. Diagnostic assessments help determine common misconceptions and their prevalence before teaching.

## Choosing Diagnostic Assessments

When current conceptual inventories don't exist or don't cover the content you need, consider creating your own:

1. **Content**: Determine what pre-existing content, knowledge, or abilities students may need for your course, or that you assume they will know.
2. **Instrument**: A feasible diagnostic assessment can focus on student self-assessment — yes/no questions about understanding, or rating scales for familiarity/confidence. Close-ended questions automate analysis; open-ended questions can capture content students find difficult.
3. **Delivery**: Automate this assessment via LMS or survey software.

## Conceptual Inventories

Conceptual Inventories (CIs) are designed to identify critical, common misconceptions and content knowledge. They are typically multiple-choice, with answers based on common misunderstandings. CIs can be administered before and after instruction to measure conceptual change.

Key caveat: CIs don't include all possible misconceptions. Students can answer correctly without understanding why. Use other formative assessments in conjunction with CIs.

Extensive catalogues of CIs exist across disciplines (biology, chemistry, physics, computer science, engineering, math, geology, astronomy).

## Using Diagnostic Assessments

- Which topics have been difficult for students in the past because of prior knowledge issues?
- Do you plan on assessing all topics at the beginning of the course, or each topic before it's taught?
- Do you have a plan for how to approach topics that involve concepts students find difficult to learn?
