# Measuring Self-Directed Learning: A Diagnostic Tool for Adult Learners

**Author**: Henry Khiat, SIM University (UniSIM), Singapore

## Context

Self-directed learning (SDL) allows adult learners to cope better with studies while fulfilling family, work, and other commitments. This study conceptualized and validated a learning diagnostic tool at SIM University (UniSIM), an adult learning institution with 14,000 students in Singapore.

## Self-Regulation and Self-Directed Learning Cycles

### Ambrose et al. (2010) Meta-Cognitive Model:
1. Assess the task at hand (goals and constraints)
2. Evaluate own knowledge and skills (strengths and weaknesses)
3. Plan approach (account for current situation)
4. Apply strategies (monitor progress)
5. Reflect on approach effectiveness (adjust and restart cycle)

### Key Findings on Self-Regulation and Performance:
- SDL level positively correlated with academic performance, motivation, and persistence
- Study skills courses that increase self-regulation are effective preparation
- Strategies can be imparted through direct instruction, guided practice, instructor feedback, peer support, and pedagogical adaptation

## The Diagnostic Tool

### Design
70 initial items designed to measure self-directed learning skill competence, evaluated on a 7-point Likert scale (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree). Content validated by faculty. Exploratory and confirmatory factor analysis with 1,960 respondents.

### Resulting 10-Factor Model (26 items)

| Factor | Domain | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Assignment Management | Ability to plan, do, and score well in assignments |
| 2 | Online Learning Proficiency | Ability to learn from online lectures |
| 3 | Stress Management | Ability to handle academic stress |
| 4 | Technical Proficiency | Ability to handle the LMS |
| 5 | Procrastination Management | Ability to manage procrastination in learning |
| 6 | Online Discussion Proficiency | Ability to do well in discussion forums |
| 7 | Seminar Learning Proficiency | Ability to learn during seminars |
| 8 | Comprehension Competence | Ability to understand readings and make study notes |
| 9 | Examination Management | Ability to plan, do, and score well in examinations |
| 10 | Time Management | Ability to utilize study time effectively |

### Tool Output
After completion, students receive a report showing:
- Mean score in each domain compared to the validation population
- Domains where the student might be weak (scores at or below the mean of low-GPA students)
- Domains where the student is doing well (top 30th percentile)

### Key Validated Insight
The tool is designed as "the first line of self-diagnosis for students to understand their own learning needs." However, it cannot be the sole mechanism — a comprehensive network of detection and intervention (faculty, counselors) supports students who are not performing well.

## Relevance to Placement and Scaffolding

This is one of the few validated diagnostic tools specifically designed for self-directed learners to identify their own strengths and weaknesses. For our purposes (pedagogical scaffolding to help learners find their best starting point), it demonstrates that:
1. A multi-factor diagnostic can be both validated and practical
2. Self-assessment dimensions extend beyond content knowledge into learning-strategy domains
3. Diagnostic output can directly map to scaffolding interventions
4. The 10-factor model offers a template for what dimensions a placement/diagnostic system should assess
